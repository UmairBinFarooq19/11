Month by Month

Gulmarg Through the Year

Gulmarg sits at 2,650m in the western Himalayas. It has two very distinct seasons — a cold, snowy winter ideal for skiing and a warm, wildflower-filled summer perfect for trekking and outdoor adventure.

December

−8°C to 2°C
❄️ Ski Season Opens

Season opens Dec 1. Snowpack building — 1–2m typical by mid-month. Good early-season powder. Cold, clear days common.

January

−12°C to −2°C
❄️ Peak Powder

Best powder month of the year. Snowpack at maximum. Coldest temperatures but lightest, driest snow. Excellent visibility.

February

−10°C to 0°C
❄️ Excellent Snow

Continues delivering outstanding conditions. Consolidated snowpack, slightly lower avalanche risk than January. Very popular month.

March

−5°C to 6°C
❄️ Spring Skiing

Snow softens through the day — firm in mornings, heavy by afternoon. Longer daylight and warmer temperatures. Quieter slopes.

April

2°C to 12°C
⛷ End of Season

Season typically closes April 10. Spring skiing — soft snow, warm sun and dramatic views. Phase 2 may close earlier.

May

8°C to 18°C
🌱 Shoulder Season

Snow melting at lower elevations. Wildflowers beginning to emerge. Gondola may still operate for sightseeing.

June

12°C to 22°C
☀️ Summer Begins

Meadows turning green. Wildflowers in bloom. Excellent trekking and outdoor conditions. Gondola Phase 2 may have snow patches.

July

14°C to 25°C
☀️ Peak Summer

Peak wildflower season. Warmest month. Perfect for trekking, camping, horse riding and photography. Occasional afternoon rain.

August

13°C to 24°C
☀️ High Summer

Continued excellent summer conditions. Alpine meadows at their finest. Good visibility and long daylight hours. Some monsoon influence.

September

9°C to 20°C
🍂 Early Autumn

Beautiful autumn colours developing. Cooler temperatures. Excellent trekking. Clear skies and outstanding mountain views.

October

2°C to 14°C
🍂 Late Autumn

Temperatures dropping. First snowfall possible at higher elevations. Resort preparations begin. Quiet and beautiful.

November

−4°C to 6°C
❄️ Pre-Season

Heavy snowfall begins. Resort preparing for opening. Not open for skiing yet but spectacular winter landscape emerging.

What to Pack — Winter Ski Trip

🧥
Base Layer

Merino wool or synthetic thermal long underwear — top and bottom. Two sets minimum.

🎿
Mid Layer

Fleece or down mid-layer for insulation. Should fit under your ski jacket comfortably.

🌨
Outer Layer

Waterproof, windproof ski jacket and trousers. Proper ski outerwear — not just a winter coat.

🧤
Gloves & Socks

Waterproof ski gloves (not standard winter gloves) and wool ski socks. Bring two pairs of each.

🪖
Helmet & Goggles

Mandatory for Phase 2 and off-piste. Available for hire but personal-fit equipment always performs better.

☀️
Sun Protection

SPF 50+ sunscreen and UV-blocking lip balm. Mountain sun reflected off snow causes rapid burning even in winter.

Plan Your Visit

Best Time to Come

❄️ Best for Skiing: January & February

January and February deliver the deepest, lightest powder of the season. Snowpack at maximum depth, cold temperatures preserving snow quality and the widest range of terrain open. Book early — these months fill fastest.

☀️ Best for Trekking: June to September

Summer transforms Gulmarg into a wildflower paradise. July and August offer peak conditions for trekking, camping and outdoor exploration with warm days, long daylight and the valley at its most colourful.
