🟢 Beginner Zone

Zone 1 — Poma Lifts

7Poma Lifts
8°–15°Gradient
200–400mRun Length

The seven poma-lift slopes distributed around Gulmarg meadow are where every ski journey properly starts. The terrain is wide, consistently groomed and forgiving enough for complete beginners to find their footing without anxiety. Most runs return to the meadow level, so there is no long traverse required to reach the lift again.

Our certified instructors run structured beginner courses exclusively on Zone 1 terrain for the first two to three days, ensuring that foundational technique — balance, stopping, turning — is firmly established before any higher terrain is considered. Children take to these slopes particularly quickly.

🔵 Intermediate Zone

Zone 2 — Gondola Phase 1

3,050mAltitude
400mVertical Drop
4kmRun Length

Gondola Phase 1 carries guests from the meadow base at 2,690m up to Kongdori station at 3,050m — a gain of 360 metres. The bowl that opens at the top is a broad, naturally contoured space with enough variety to keep intermediate skiers engaged across a full day of laps.

After two or three days of solid Zone 1 instruction, most students are ready to take their first runs here. The descent back to the meadow base is approximately 4km and involves a satisfying mix of pitch changes and open terrain. Zone 2 is where technical skiing truly begins to click for most people.

🔴 Advanced Zone

Zone 3 — Gondola Phase 2 & Chair Lift

3,980mPeak Altitude
500–900mVertical Gain
7kmMax Run

Gondola Phase 2 ascends from Kongdori to the Apharwat ridge at 3,980m — one of the highest accessible ski points in India. A separate chair lift offers an intermediate ascent to the mid-mountain zone. The terrain at this level is significantly steeper, with gradients between 20° and 45° depending on the line selected.

This is where Gulmarg's reputation for legendary powder skiing was built. Lines on this face hold snow exceptionally well after storms, and the sustained vertical makes for long, memorable descents. A helmet and prior experience on Zone 2 terrain are necessary before committing to Phase 2. Our guides accompany all guests on this zone.

Zone 4 — Gulmarg Backcountry

Mount Apharwat's backcountry terrain at 4,200m is the centrepiece of Gulmarg's global ski reputation. Vast bowls stream down the north and south flanks of the main ridge, with lines that drop between 900 and 1,500 vertical metres through untouched Himalayan powder.

Gulmarg sits in the western Himalayas within the Pir Panjal range and receives snowfall patterns that produce some of Asia's most consistent deep powder. Between December and February, base depths of four to five metres are common, and the north-facing bowls hold cold, light snow for days after a storm.

The terrain beyond the gondola stations is classified as backcountry and is not patrolled or avalanche-controlled by resort operations. Any guest venturing into Zone 4 must be accompanied by a certified guide with current avalanche safety credentials.

⚠️

Backcountry terrain in Gulmarg is unpatrolled and avalanche risk is real. This zone is strictly for experienced skiers travelling with a certified guide. Our team holds AIARE Level 1 and NZ Avalanche Safety certifications. We will not take unprepared guests beyond the lift system.

Drung Valley Lines

Narrower spurs and chutes that channel east towards the Drung Valley. Technically demanding with serious avalanche terrain requiring thorough assessment before entry.

A touring route along the main Apharwat ridge that gives access to several subsidiary bowls rarely tracked even in busy season. Outstanding panoramic views throughout.

A natural extension off the Phase 1 bowl into ungroomed terrain below the main ridge — a good introduction to off-piste conditions for confident intermediate skiers.

December

Early season — snowpack building. Some lines open, variable conditions. Excellent for early-season powder hunting.

Peak powder month. Deepest snowpack, coldest temperatures, lightest snow. Best overall backcountry conditions of the year.

🟢 Best
February

Continues to deliver outstanding conditions. Snowpack fully consolidated and stable, reducing avalanche risk versus January.

Snow softens through the day. Morning sessions on firm-tracked powder, afternoons can be heavy. Longer daylight for touring.
