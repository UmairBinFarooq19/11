Ski Runs & Lines

Nursery Slope

🟢 Beginner

Primary first-day slope — flat gradient, wide and groomed. Poma-lift accessed from the meadow base.

8°–12°200mPoma lift

Highlands Park

🟢 Beginner

Longest beginner poma run — a great confidence builder after the nursery slope.

12°350mPoma lift

Gondola Bowl (Kongdori)

🔵 Intermediate

Main Phase 1 descent from 3,050m. Broad bowl with a satisfying 4km run back to base.

10°–18°4kmGondola P1

Mary's Shoulder

🔵 Intermediate+

Chair lift accessed — more challenging pitch into the upper Kongdori area.

15°–25°2kmChair lift

Apharwat Face

🔴 Advanced

Phase 2 headline run — 3,980m summit, sustained steep descent. Guide recommended.

20°–45°7kmGondola P2
⚠️ Out-of-bounds terrain is unpatrolled. A certified guide and full avalanche safety kit are mandatory. Check today's avalanche forecast before going off-piste.

Locker's Left

🔴 Advanced

One of Gulmarg's most popular out-of-bounds lines. Drops left off the Phase 2 ridge into a sustained powder bowl.

25°–38°Guide advised

Locker's Right

🔴 Advanced

Slightly more technical than Left with a narrower entry before opening into a wider bowl below.

28°–42°Guide advised

The Back

⚫ Expert

North side of Apharwat ridge. Serious avalanche exposure — guide and full safety gear mandatory.

35°–50°Guide mandatory

Far Far Side

⚫ Expert

Most remote line at Gulmarg. Rarely tracked even in peak season. Vehicle return required.

30°–48°Vehicle return

Monkey Hill

🔴 Advanced

Fan favourite for advanced skiers — excellent pitch, reliable powder, gondola return.

22°–35°Gondola return

To Tangmarg

⚫ Expert

Full resort-to-town descent — 15km+ from Apharwat all the way to Tangmarg village.

Mixed15km+Taxi return
⚠️ All backcountry terrain is avalanche uncontrolled. Guide, transceiver, probe and shovel are mandatory. Check today's forecast →

North Bowl

⚫ Expert

Best consistent backcountry line — broad, north-facing, holds powder for days after a storm.

30°–45°900–1,200m vert

Drung Valley Lines

⚫ Expert

Narrower spurs east toward Drung — technically demanding with serious avalanche terrain.

35°–55°1,200–1,500m vert

Sharkfin Touring Route

⚫ Expert

Ridge touring line giving access to multiple subsidiary bowls rarely tracked even in busy season.

20°–40°Full day

North Border Lines

⚫ Expert

Most remote terrain — rarely tracked, demanding navigation and exceptional powder.

40°–50°1,500m vert
